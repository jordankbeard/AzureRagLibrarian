The Quiet Hours Operating Guide

Brackenford is a small riverside town known for its clocktower, night market, and careful approach to shared rest. The town council created the Quiet Hours programme after a long season of overwork left shopkeepers, teachers, and engineers exhausted.

Quiet Hours begin at 8:30 p.m. every weekday. During this time, public bells are silenced, market stalls close, and non-urgent maintenance waits until morning. Emergency services, medical teams, and transport staff continue to operate as normal.

The clocktower uses two pendulums. The day pendulum keeps the town schedule moving from 6:00 a.m. to 8:30 p.m. The evening pendulum slows the chime mechanism and switches the tower lanterns from white to amber.

Lysa Calder, the tower keeper, is responsible for inspecting the pendulums every Friday. She records gear wear, lantern status, and the exact time the evening pendulum engages. If the mechanism drifts by more than two minutes, she files a maintenance note with the council.

Professor Thalen Mire maintains the public archive. His records show that productivity improved after Quiet Hours were restored because people made fewer mistakes, slept more consistently, and planned work with better focus.

The Merchant Guild originally objected to the programme because evening trading hours were reduced. After three months, the guild reported lower spoilage, fewer accounting errors, and higher customer satisfaction during daytime trading.

Residents can request an exception for festivals, repairs, or visiting delegations. Exceptions must be approved by both the council clerk and the tower keeper. Approved exceptions are posted in the archive window before noon on the day they apply.

Common questions:

- Quiet Hours start at 8:30 p.m. on weekdays.
- The evening pendulum changes the tower lanterns to amber.
- Lysa Calder inspects the clocktower pendulums every Friday.
- Professor Thalen Mire maintains the public archive.
- Emergency and medical services are not paused during Quiet Hours.
